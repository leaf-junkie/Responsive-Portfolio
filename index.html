About Me

Born in Tallahassee, Florida, I moved to North Carolina in 2008 to study biology. I fell in loved with botany and heavily focused on plant sciences during my undergraduate years. As soon as I graduated with my B.S., I delved into graduate school wherein I studied plant-insect interactions.

I received my M.S. in Ecology and Evolutionary Biology from Appalachian State University in 2016. My research explored the effects of spatial scale and genotypic diversity of Solidago altissima on the associated insect pollinator community. Simply put, I examined the differences in the phytochemistry among individual plants and how those differences influenced pollinators.

I am also an artist/maker and small business owner, having started my sole proprietorship of Calathea Collection in the summer of 2018. Everything that I create is related to plants in some way. I have been painting with acrylic since 2004 and have more recently begun experimenting with watercolor and gouache; I use cement to make small planters (this is quickly becoming my favorite aspect of my business!), and I make macrame plant hangers. You could say that my brand is an ode to the plant kingdom.
